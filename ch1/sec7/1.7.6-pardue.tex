\paragraph{}
\begin{exercise}
Suppose the functors and natural transformations
\[\begin{array}{ccccc}
\xymatrix{\sC\ar@<.5ex>[r]^F & \sD\ar@<.5ex>[l]^G} && \eta\colon1_\sC\cong GF&& \ep\colon FG\cong 1_\sD\\
\xymatrix{\sD\ar@<.5ex>[r]^{F^\prime} & \sE\ar@<.5ex>[l]^{G^\prime}} && \eta^\prime\colon1_\sD\cong G^\prime F^\prime&& \ep^\prime\colon F^\prime G^\prime\cong 1_\sE\\
\end{array}
\]
define equivalences of categories $\sC\simeq\sD$ and $\sD\simeq\sE$. Prove (again) that there is a composite equivalence of categories $\sC\simeq\sE$ by defining composite natural isomorphisms $1_\sC\cong GG^\prime F^\prime F$ and $F^\prime FGG^\prime\cong 1_\sE$.
\end{exercise}

\begin{proof}
First, we would like to make an additional remark on Exercise 1.7.ii. In that
exercise, given functors $F\colon\sC\rightarrow\sD$,
$H\colon\sD\rightarrow\sE$, $K\colon\sD\rightarrow\sE$ and
$L\colon\sE\rightarrow\sF$ and a natural transformation $\beta\colon
H\Rightarrow K$, we obtained a natural transformation $L\beta F\colon
LHF\Rightarrow LKF$, the \emph{whiskered composite} of $\beta$ with $L$ and
$F$, given by $(L\beta F)_c=L\beta_{Fc}$.

We claim that if $\beta$ is a natural isomorphism, then so is $L\beta F$.
Indeed, if $\beta$ is a natural isomorphism then $\beta_d$ is an isomorphism
for every object $d$ of $\sD$. So, $\beta_{Fc}$ is an isomorphism for every
object $c$ of $\sC$. By Lemma 1.3.8, $L\beta_{Fc}$ is an isomorphism as well,
showing that $L\beta F$ is a natural isomorphism as claimed.

Similarly, note that the vertical composition $\alpha\cdot\beta$ of two natural isomorphisms is a natural isomorphism since its component morphisms $(\alpha\cdot\beta)_c=\alpha_c\beta_c$ are compositions of isomorphisms.

Now to the exercise at hand. From
\[\xymatrix{\sC\ar[r]^F &\sD\ar@/^1pc/[rr]^{1_\sD}\ar@/_1pc/[rr]_{G^\prime F^\prime}&\Downarrow\eta^\prime & \sD\ar[r]^G & \sC}\]
we obtain the natural isomorphism $G\eta^\prime F\colon GF\Rightarrow GG^\prime
F^\prime F$. Thus, we have a natural isomorphism $(G\eta^\prime
F)\cdot\eta\colon1_\sC\Rightarrow GG^\prime F^\prime F$.

For the other direction, we consider the whiskered composite
\[\xymatrix{\sE\ar[r]^{G^\prime} &\sD\ar@/^1pc/[rr]^{FG}\ar@/_1pc/[rr]_{1_\sD}&\Downarrow\ep & \sD\ar[r]^{F^\prime} & \sE}\]
giving the natural isomorphism $F^\prime\ep G^\prime\colon F^\prime
FGG^\prime\Rightarrow F^\prime G^\prime$. Vertical composition with
$\ep^\prime$ gives the natural isomorphism $\ep^\prime\cdot(F^\prime\ep
G^\prime)\colon F^\prime FGG^\prime\Rightarrow 1_\sE$ as required.
\end{proof}
